Minimum Wage and CPI in the U.S. from 1968 to 2020

Over the years, both the Consumer Price Index (CPI) and the minimum wage in the United States have been on the rise. The CPI measures the monthly change in prices paid by U.S. consumers, reflecting inflation and cost of living adjustments. The U.S. has a national minimum wage set by federal law, but each state can also have its own minimum wage, which can differ from the federal level.

In this interactive slideshow, you'll explore the changes in the national minimum wage over the years, view a scatterplot of the state minimum wages over time, and examine a scatter plot of the Consumer Price Index changes over the years.
